The problem

Cleaning companies run on paper, calls and WhatsApp groups

Verified research: Nigerian field-service businesses without software coordinate jobs by phone call, schedule staff in WhatsApp groups, and track work on paper job cards. Money leaks everywhere.

Paper job cards get lost

No record of what was cleaned, who did it, or what the client agreed to pay. Disputes end in write-offs.

WhatsApp groups aren't a schedule

Who is at which site? Did the 8am team show up? Owners burn hours every day chasing staff by call.

Cash and transfers go untracked

Field-collected cash, unmatched bank transfers, forgotten invoices — and the global tools can't collect naira at all.

Built on verified research, not guesses

We ran a deep, multi-source study of the Nigerian cleaning industry — every claim below survived adversarial fact-checking against primary sources. (Claims that failed — including every published market-size figure — were thrown out.)

Verified · payments gap
Jobber, ZenMaid, Housecall Pro and ServiceTitan cannot collect money in Nigeria. No Paystack, no Flutterwave, no naira, no USSD. Mesjia's payment rails are the moat.
Verified · Android-first
~83% of Nigerian mobile devices run Android (Statcounter, Jun 2026). Mesjia ships as a lightweight, low-data Android PWA for owners and cleaners.
Verified · trust problem
Hiring trustworthy staff is the #1 recurring struggle for Nigerian cleaning owners. Mesjia builds vetting in: NIN/ID records, guarantors, background-check status, client-facing verified profiles.
Verified · how firms quote
Residential cleaning is quoted by room count in naira ("2-bed from ₦20k…"). Mesjia's quote builder uses property-type templates with adjustable floors.
Verified · commercial demand
Offices, malls and government buildings dominate demand and need daily recurring cleaning. Mesjia treats recurring contracts as first-class: auto-scheduled jobs, monthly invoicing.
Verified · WhatsApp workflows
WhatsApp is the default workspace of African SMEs. Mesjia doesn't fight it — quotes, invoices, reminders and verified-cleaner profiles all send through WhatsApp in one tap.

The product

26 features, shipped in 4 phases

Each phase is piloted on a real cleaning company before the next begins — the founder's own business runs on Mesjia from Phase 1.

Phase 1 · Operate

Owner runs the business

  • Client CRM + sites & access notes
  • Job calendar & digital job cards
  • 3 job types with checklists
  • Room-count quote builder (₦)
  • Invoices + WhatsApp share
  • Owner dashboard
Exit: a real week scheduled & invoiced in Mesjia
Phase 2 · Field

Cleaners on the app

  • Cleaner "My Jobs" view
  • GPS check-in / check-out
  • Before & after photos
  • On-site checklist ticking
  • Staff vetting: ID + guarantor
  • Verified-cleaner client share
Exit: a job completed end-to-end on a cleaner's phone
Phase 3 · Money

Payments that fit Nigeria

  • Paystack links: card · transfer · USSD
  • Field cash logging + confirm
  • Deposits & part-payments
  • Auto payment reminders
  • Recurring contracts → auto jobs
  • Transfer auto-reconciliation
Exit: a client pays a Mesjia invoice via Paystack
Phase 4 · Scale

Grow beyond one company

  • Attendance & payroll runs
  • Public booking link
  • Client ratings & reports
  • Multi-site rollups
  • WhatsApp Business API
  • SaaS billing & plan limits
Exit: a second company onboards self-serve, paid
