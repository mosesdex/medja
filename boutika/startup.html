01 · The problem

She loses money she can't see

A boutique owner runs a real business on memory, a notebook, and WhatsApp. The consequences are measurable — and they're the pains a management app removes.

🕳️

Money lost to blind management

Memory‑based management causes "undetected revenue leakages, stock mismanagement, and unrecorded debts."

✅ Verified · IRJMS 2026
🚨

Staff theft undefended

Many Nigerian SMEs have "no preventive measures against employee theft."

✅ Verified · JFC 2018
📒

Credit sales pile up untracked

Selling on credit is "a Nigerian small business reality" — receivables scatter across notebooks and chats until unpaid debt threatens the shop.

🟡 Reported · SimpleBooks
💸

Existing software out of reach

Accountants and accounting software are "prohibitive"; foreign tools demand documentation discipline owners lack.

✅ Verified
She does not want accounting. She wants to stop losing money to stock, staff, and debtors — without becoming a bookkeeper. That's what we build first.

03 · Who it serves

Owner first, attendant second, buyer eventually

👩🏾‍💼

The owner‑operator

1 shop and/or active Instagram/WhatsApp page, 1–3 staff, restocking on trips (Balogun/Yaba/Onitsha/Aba; Istanbul/Guangzhou/Dubai/UK). Primary — Phase 1.

🧍🏾‍♀️

The shop attendant

Logs sales under her own PIN — turning staff from a theft risk into an accountable node. Secondary — Phase 1.

🛒

The buyer

Discovers verified boutiques in a trust‑engineered marketplace; pays on delivery / pay‑to‑release. Eventual — Phase 3+.

07 · Go‑to‑market

Concierge in, recover money, convert, then turn on selling

Step 1

Concierge Phase 0

Hand‑onboard 15–25 boutiques in one cluster (a Lagos plaza or an active Instagram‑vendor circle). Log their stock and debtors for them; watch what they actually use.

Step 2

Land free, beat the notebook

The wedge is faster sale/stock/debtor logging — not a website.

Step 3

Recover money → convert to Pro

The upgrade trigger is visible debt collected via WhatsApp reminders.

Step 4

Turn on selling

Once she trusts Boutika with her numbers, the storefront is one toggle.

Step 5

Cluster‑by‑cluster density

Boutiques refer boutiques within a market/plaza/niche; density feeds the eventual marketplace.

Channels: Instagram/WhatsApp vendor communities, market‑association partnerships, fashion micro‑influencers, and referral loops.
08 · Roadmap

Phases with exit tests

Each phase must pass its exit test before the next begins — the Medja/Handwork discipline.

Phase 0 · 4–6 wks · minimal code

Validation

Concierge‑onboard 15–25 boutiques; log stock + debtors manually.

Exit: ≥10 owners say "don't take this away," ≥5 verbally commit to ₦3,500/mo after seeing recovered debt/shrinkage.
Phase 1 · 8–10 wks

Trust‑winning core

Ship Tier 0: one‑tap sale, variant inventory, debtor ledger + reminders, money view, staff PINs, offline PWA.

Exit: 50 active boutiques logging daily; median core action <5s; ≥30% free→Pro conversion in cohort 1.
Phase 2 · 8–12 wks

Selling attached

Instant storefront, WhatsApp checkout, reconciling payments, social sync.

Exit: ≥40% of Pro users turn on a storefront; first reconciled online GMV; storefront users retain better.
Phase 3 · 12+ wks

Moat: analytics + marketplace beta

Restock‑trip planner; invite‑only marketplace in 1–2 dense clusters with pay‑on‑delivery.

Exit: buyers complete repeat purchases; verified‑seller trust beats cold social selling; supply density feels "full."
Phase 4 · thereafter

Scale

New clusters/cities; layaway; naira‑safe restock financing underwritten on real history.

09 · Open questions

To resolve in Phase 0

Will owners pay ₦3,500/mo after recovering visible debt/shrinkage they can see?
Thrift/okrika one‑of‑one items break the variant matrix — separate mode, or out of v1 scope?
Cheapest WhatsApp path that still auto‑captures the order (Business API vs. click‑to‑chat)?
How many trusted, active boutiques before a buyer marketplace is worth launching?
Can we auto‑reconcile a bank transfer to an invoice without full open‑banking access?
Relationship to the portfolio: Boutika is the third vertical after Medja (cleaning) and Handwork (building trades). It reuses the shared core — naira payments, WhatsApp messaging, Android‑first PWA, staff/role management, offline sync — but the catalog/inventory model is boutique‑specific. It has the clearest proven comparable (Bumpa) and the clearest verified failure to learn from (Kippa).
